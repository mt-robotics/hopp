# **Humans of Phnom Penh**

Humans of Phnom Penh is a platform that is dedicated to showcasing the rich diversity and culture of the city of Phnom Penh, Cambodia. Through a series of interviews, photographs, and videos, the platform aims to bring together the unique stories of the city’s people and create a space for empathy, understanding, and dialogue.

[About Us](https://humansofphnompenh.com/about-us/)

Phnom Penh is a city with a rich history that is reflected in its unique architecture, cuisine, and customs. At the same time, the city is also rapidly evolving and embracing new ideas and opportunities. Humans of Phnom Penh captures this unique blend of tradition and modernity by featuring a diverse range of people and their stories.

Whether you are a local resident or a visitor to the city, Humans of Phnom Penh invites you to join us on a journey of discovery and exploration. By sharing the stories of the people who make this city what it is, we hope to create a deeper understanding of its culture, history, and future.

The platform features regular interviews with people from all walks of life, including artists, entrepreneurs, activists, and everyday residents. Through these interviews, we gain insights into their lives, passions, and perspectives on the city and its culture.

Accompanying these interviews are high-quality photographs and videos that capture the essence of the city and its people. From bustling markets to serene riverside neighborhoods, the platform offers a window into the many different facets of Phnom Penh.

[Stories](https://humansofphnompenh.com/stories/)

At the heart of Humans of Phnom Penh is the belief that everyone has a story worth sharing. By creating a space where these stories can be told and heard, we hope to build bridges between communities and foster a deeper appreciation for the diversity and humanity of this incredible city.

To explore the latest interviews, photographs, and videos, visit the Humans of Phnom Penh website at www.humansofphnompenh.com. Whether you are a lifelong resident or a curious traveler, we invite you to join us on this journey of discovery and celebration.

# About Us

Humans of Phnom Penh is a platform that is dedicated to showcasing the rich diversity and culture of the city of Phnom Penh, Cambodia. Through a series of interviews, photographs, and videos, the platform aims to bring together the unique stories of the city’s people and create a space for empathy, understanding, and dialogue.

# MISSION

Our mission at Humans of Phnom Penh is to highlight the diverse and captivating stories of the people who call this city their home. Through our platform, we aim to foster a greater sense of empathy and understanding among our audience, promoting dialogue and celebrating the cultural richness of Phnom Penh.

# Vision

Our vision is to become a leading platform for showcasing the diversity and cultural heritage of Phnom Penh, while promoting social cohesion, inclusivity and understanding among its people.

# Objectives

TO SHOWCASE THE UNIQUE AND CAPTIVATING STORIES OF THE PEOPLE OF PHNOM PENH THROUGH A SERIES OF INTERVIEWS, PHOTOGRAPHS, AND VIDEOS.

TO FOSTER EMPATHY AND UNDERSTANDING AMONG OUR AUDIENCE, PROMOTING GREATER SOCIAL COHESION AND INCLUSIVITY WITHIN THE CITY.

TO CELEBRATE THE CULTURAL RICHNESS AND DIVERSITY OF PHNOM PENH, HIGHLIGHTING THE DIFFERENT TRADITIONS, BELIEFS, AND PRACTICES THAT MAKE IT SUCH A SPECIAL PLACE.

TO CREATE A SPACE FOR DIALOGUE AND EXCHANGE OF IDEAS, ENCOURAGING OUR AUDIENCE TO ENGAGE WITH THE STORIES AND PERSPECTIVES SHARED ON OUR PLATFORM.

TO PROMOTE TOURISM AND CULTURAL EXCHANGE BY SHOWCASING THE HIDDEN GEMS AND LOCAL EXPERIENCES THAT PHNOM PENH HAS TO OFFER.

We are looking for volunteers and interns to join our team and help us to achieve our mission. Whether you are interested in writing, photography, videography, or social media, we have a role for you.

# Pitch Your Pal: Phnom Penh

# **Less swiping. More stories.**

A live social night where friends pitch their single friends using short, funny presentations — followed by drinks, laughs, and real conversations.

Dating apps are exhausting. Speed dating is awkward.  
Pitch Your Pal turns dating into entertainment — and lets friendship do the talking.

[Register to Attend](https://humansofphnompenh.com/pitch-your-pal-phnom-penh/#register)

[Pitch a Friend](https://humansofphnompenh.com/pitch-your-pal-phnom-penh/#pitch)

## So… what exactly happens?

Pitch Your Pal is a live event where people get on stage and pitch their single friends using **3–5 minute slide decks**.

It’s funny, warm, and respectful. Think storytelling, not selling. After the pitches, the room opens up for a relaxed **Mix & Mingle**.

You come for the show. You stay for the conversations.

### Why people love it

Introductions feel natural when they come from friends

You learn more from a story than a profile

The vibe is social, not desperate

Works for both locals and expats

A great night — even without “the one”

### How the night runs

- **Doors Open:** Arrive, grab a drink
- **Pitches:** 8–12 short presentations
- **Mix & Mingle:** Music up, talk naturally

### Good vibes only

All presentations are screened

Consent from pitched friend is mandatory

No explicit or humiliating content

Zero tolerance for harassment

MC controls tone and pacing

## Pitch Guidelines

Read before submitting

### Presentation Basics

- 3–5 minutes per pitch
- 5–8 slides maximum
- Funny, warm, and respectful

### What Works Well

- Stories
- Personality traits
- Hobbies and quirks
- Green flags

### What’s Not Allowed

- Explicit or sexual content
- Embarrassing photos
- Past relationship drama
- Private messages or screenshots
- Anything your friend wouldn’t laugh at themselves

Our goal is connection — not humiliation.

#### Disclaimer

Pitch Your Pal is a social entertainment event. We do not guarantee matches or outcomes — only a great night out.

# Principal Daniel Peou Creates a Family Atmosphere at Furness Middle School in South Philadelphia

Walking into Horace Furness School in South Philadelphia feels like stepping into a loving, supportive family, thanks in large part to the leadership of Principal Daniel Peou.

Peou arrived in Philadelphia as a young immigrant from Cambodia in 1981, and Furness Middle School became his home. Today, he is the school’s principal and has transformed Furness into a hub for the community.

Under Peou’s leadership, Furness has become a point of pride for everyone involved. He purposely cultivates a family-like environment through an open-door policy for students who need support, working through problems and conflicts together, hosting events, creating community partnerships, and more. Peou and his staff make an effort to greet students in the morning, check on them during the day, and walk them outside during dismissal.

Being an immigrant himself, Peou understands the challenges that students who are English learners face. Fully half of Furness’s 750 students are English learners, with no fewer than 18 different languages spoken at their homes. Peou is committed to providing them with a safe and nurturing environment that encourages them to thrive.

Peou is also passionate about working with children, a passion he discovered while doing volunteer work as a classroom assistant. Despite the challenges of returning to grad school for an education degree, he persevered and became a bilingual counseling assistant, a teacher at Eliza Kirkbride Elementary School, and a climate manager at John Taggart Elementary School. He returned to Furness as an assistant principal in 2008 and became principal in 2012.  
One of Peou’s many strengths is his ability to work with anyone as long as it’s for the benefit of the students. He has formed partnerships with different organizations, including the University of Pennsylvania’s library to raise money to purchase new books for the Furness library. Peou’s dry sense of humor also helps him connect with new students and break down walls if they are being aloof.

Furness’s sense of community extends far beyond the school walls. During the pandemic, Peou and teachers dropped materials and food off at students’ homes if they needed it. Peou and his staff took over the distribution of Thanksgiving gift baskets from the Philadelphia Police Department and found greater success by doing so.

Peou sums up Furness’s sense of community by saying, “Once you come to Furness, you are family.” We are thankful for principals like Daniel Peou who go above and beyond to create warm and supportive environments for students.

# Leth Oun: A Journey from the Paddy Fields to the U.S. Secret Service

#### “Explore the inspiring journey of Leth Oun – from the rice fields of Battambang, Cambodia, surviving Khmer Rouge’s brutal regime, to making history in the U.S. Secret Service. His story is a testament to human resilience and the unwavering pursuit of a better life.”

From the throes of despair and misfortune, strong individuals can emerge who, through their story, inspire the world with their resilience and unbowed spirit. Such is the story of Leth Oun. His journey from Battambang’s rice fields in Cambodia to the esteemed ranks of the U.S Secret Service is nothing short of extraordinary.

## Battambang – The Early Years

In the modest city of Battambang in western Cambodia, Leth Oun spent his childhood where happiness was not associated with material wealth. Despite their financial hardships, Oun’s family managed to provide him with education and a nurturing home environment. His father, serving in the Lon Nol government, acted as that beacon of hope and support until the Khmer Rouge took over in April 1975.

## The Dark Times Under the Khmer Rouge

The arrival of Khmer Rouge turned Leth Oun’s world topsy-turvy. His father disappeared, and he was forced into the grim occupation of sowing rice in the “killing fields.” However, the tale of Oun’s survival against all odds lies in his mother’s tenacity and quick wit, concealing their family origins behind fabricated names.

## Survival Amidst Chaos

The 1979 Vietnamese invasion didn’t bring the hope for peace they longed for. Instead, it prompted Leth Oun and his mother to undertake precarious trips between Battambang and the Thai border. Risking life and limb, they procured food and resold it back home, thus ensuring their survival. It was at the Thai border, within the uncertainty and desolation of refugee camps, that they found sanctuary. Later, they were granted refugee status and a ticket to rebuild their lives, in the United States.

## The Beacon of Hope: U.S. Secret Service

In the U.S., Leth Oun defied all odds to join the esteemed U.S. Secret Service, making history as the first Cambodian to join its venerated ranks. His responsibilities are far from trivial, protecting presidents, vice presidents, and their families. He takes particular interest in the K9 unit, where he trains dogs for bomb detection – a crucial weapon in the security apparatus. Oun now “walks the White House like it’s home” – a realm far removed from the paddy fields of his youth.

## Chronicle of Triumph: A Refugee’s American Dream

To further honor his remarkable journey, Leth Oun chronicled his story in the book “A Refugee’s American Dream: From the Killing Fields of Cambodia to the U.S. Secret Service.” With the help of Joe Samuel Starnes, Oun narrates his powerful story – of survival, cunning, and success. It serves as an enduring testament to the strength of human spirit and the unwavering pursuit of a better life.

From the killing fields to protecting the powerful, Leth Oun’s journey embodies hope and strength, proving that one’s past does not have to dictate their future. His story provides comfort and inspiration to millions around the world – that with grit and determination, any hurdle can be surmounted, and any dream, no matter how distant, can be pursued with success.

# A New Era for Cambodian Disability Rights: The Historical Appointment of Chap To

#### “Explore the historical appointment of Chap To, a visually impaired individual, as undersecretary of state in Cambodia. Discover his inspirational journey and the positive impact his leadership is expected to bring for disability rights and inclusion in Cambodia.”

In a groundbreaking move, King Norodom Sihamoni issued a royal decree appointing Chap To, a visually impaired individual, as undersecretary of state to the Ministry of Social Affairs, Veterans and Youth Rehabilitation. This appointment, confirmed by the Disability Action Council (DAC), marks the first time a person with visual impairment has assumed such a senior role. This monumental decision sends a powerful message about the increasing inclusion of people with disabilities in Cambodia.

## The Inspirational Journey of Chap To

Chap To’s accomplishments and distinctive career path embody his passion for championing the rights and welfare of people living with disabilities. Not only has he actively fought for a more inclusive and accessible society on a national level, but he has also made a significant impact on the lives of countless individuals.

Beyond his new position, To is well-respected as the president and founder of the Samneang Music Association for the Disabled. This organization places him at the heart of endeavors aimed at enhancing the independence and welfare of disabled Cambodians.

## Overcoming Obstacles and Empowering Communities

Born in 1993 to farming parents in Svay Rieng province, To’s path was laden with challenges. However, his perseverance shone through, and he completed his bachelor’s degree in commerce in 2016\. As head of the music association, he now oversees around 700 individuals, as highlighted by the DAC on June 30.

The government’s focus on disability rights is gaining momentum, demonstrated by the widespread attention given to individuals with disabilities. The DAC commended the country’s efforts, citing the implementation of laws and standards specifically designed to protect the disabled and facilitate their independent living.

## A Promising Shift in Employment Opportunities

An encouraging trend pointed out by the DAC is the growing number of disabled individuals being motivated to seek employment opportunities in both the private and public sectors. This increasing emphasis on including people with disabilities in various aspects of society showcases Cambodia’s growing commitment to equal opportunity and social integration.

Chap To’s historical appointment serves as a testament to the progress being made in the field of disability rights. As society continues to evolve, we celebrate the achievements of Chap To and eagerly anticipate the positive impact his leadership will bring to Cambodia and its citizens.

Boret Ream hails from Banteay Meanchey Province, where he was born into a farming family. His father also served as a commune officer. As a waiter, he learned about food, different cultures and built the confidence to interact with people from various parts of the world. Boret identified this gap and decided to act on it.

He started conducting research and meeting with tourists. A couple from the UK mentioned that it was easy in Bangkok because most day tour agencies were online, and they could book before visiting.
